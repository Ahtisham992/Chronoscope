# Name & identity

## The name

**Chronoscope**

*chrono-* (time) + *-scope* (an instrument for viewing). A chronoscope is, literally, "a device for looking through time." That is exactly the product: point it at a domain, look through 25 years of it.

## Tagline options

Primary:
> **Watch the internet grow up.**

Alternates (pick per context — landing page hero vs. social share caption):
- Paste a URL. Watch 25 years happen in 15 seconds.
- Every website has a childhood. This is where you watch it.
- History has a scrubber now.

## Why this name works

- **Not derivative of "Wayback."** The product is built on top of the Wayback Machine's data, but the name doesn't borrow its branding — it stands as its own thing, which matters both legally (no trademark confusion with the Internet Archive) and for cutting through — "yet another wayback wrapper" is a weaker pitch than "Chronoscope."
- **Says what it does without saying "archive."** "Archive," "history," and "wayback" all sound like reference tools you'd use once and forget. "Scope" implies an instrument you look through for the pleasure of looking — closer to a telescope or kaleidoscope than a database query tool. That reframes the product from *utility* to *toy*, which is the correct frame for something meant to be shared for fun.
- **Short, ownable, brandable.** Two clean syllables per half, no ambiguity on spelling once seen, `.app` / `.com` variants are realistically securable (`chronoscope.app`, `getchronoscope.com`, `trychronoscope.com`).
- **Logo-friendly.** The word visually contains a lens metaphor (-scope) and a clock metaphor (chrono-) — both are easy, non-cliché shapes to build a mark from without resorting to a literal hourglass or literal camera icon.

## Logo concept

A simple circular aperture (lens ring) with a thin radial line sweeping inside it like a clock hand — but the "hand" is a scrub-bar tick, not a clock hand. This visually says "lens + timeline" in one shape without drawing an actual clock. Rendered as a single-color mark, it should work as a favicon at 16px.

## Voice & personality

Chronoscope talks like a museum guide who is secretly delighted by the exhibit, not like a SaaS dashboard. Confident, a little wry, never corporate. It takes the *subject matter* (internet history) seriously and treats *itself* lightly.

| Chronoscope sounds like | Chronoscope never sounds like |
|---|---|
| "Amazon in 1999 had a bookstore's font size problems." | "Optimize your web archival workflow." |
| "312 snapshots. 27 years. One button." | "Leverage our platform to unlock insights." |
| "This one aged badly. Watch." | "Please enter a valid URL to proceed." |
